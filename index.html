Award-Winning Author Tere Rodríguez-Nora Releases "Pepi's Trial" - A Story of Mutual Respect and Empathy

Award-winning author Tere Rodríguez-Nora introduces her latest children's book, "Pepi's Trial," a heartwarming tale that follows a young protagonist's journey through challenges and self-discovery, teaching valuable lessons about perseverance and personal growth.

In "Pepi's Trial," readers follow the compelling story of Pepi as they navigate through various challenges and learn important life lessons. Through beautiful illustrations and engaging storytelling, the book addresses themes of courage, determination, and the power of believing in oneself.

This thoughtfully crafted story is perfect for young readers, offering them a relatable character and valuable insights into handling life's trials with grace and resilience. The book combines engaging narrative with important messages about respecting others and acknowledging differences.

Key Features:

  • Engaging storytelling that captures young readers' attention
  • Beautiful illustrations that bring Pepi's world to life
  • Important lessons about personal growth and respect
  • Perfect for reading together and discussing life's challenges

About the Author

Tere Rodriguez-Nora is an Award-Winning Author and Mental Health Therapist based in Central Florida. Her stories are inspired by the experiences of her sons and her own childhood memories. She is the acclaimed author of several children's books, including "Kikiwi y los desperdicios en el fondo del mar," "En busca de la paz," and "Las estrellas de los Reyes Magos" (International Latino Book Award, first place, 2016).

As a Mental Health Therapist, Tere works helping people in the Central Florida area, bringing her unique perspective and understanding of children's emotional needs to her writing. Her work has been featured in textbooks, and she has been a two-time collaborator of Tri-Anthology LuzDelMes.

About the Illustrator

Claudia Sofia Quiñones Flores is a multidisciplinary artist. The illustrator works as creative director and set designer in various audiovisual productions in Puerto Rico. In addition, she has illustrated and designed visuals for projects in multiple formats. Her debut in children's literature comes with "Pepi's Trial".
